As part of Current's complete rebrand initiative, the financial technology company sought to overhaul its website design to align with its new brand identity, created by world-renowned agency, Collins.

Current engaged Jae Yoon Studio as its website design partner, to help them with content strategy, UX, visual design and motion prototypes.

Organizing all of Current's offerings and benefits

As one of the initial steps of the project, we created an information architecture around Current's vast array of offerings. This was an important step as it allowed us to create a clear and concise hierarchy of products and offerings, which would ultimately serve as the backbone of the marketing website.

With a clear sense of product hierarchy, customers could easily navigate the website and understand the various offerings without feeling overwhelmed or confused.

Tangible goods

To translate all of the research and work done in the beginning phase into tangible deliverables, we designed wireframes that clearly emphasized the prioritization of content, information architecture, and UX.

These renderings served as the final blueprints for the online presence strategy, which could be shared among leadership and stakeholders.

Bringing everything together

We utilized the new brand identity by Collins and the above wireframes to start on the visual designs.

Our design approach included the use of motion design capabilities to fully demonstrate our vision of the website.

Given the prevalence of mobile usage among Current's audience, we made sure that the website was not only functional and visually appealing on mobile, but also conveyed the same level of storytelling and brand identity as on desktop.
